## Pitcher (7 scenarios)

### 1. First Batter of the Game
- **Difficulty:** 1 (Rookie)
- **Concept:** Getting ahead in the count is a pitcher's biggest advantage
- **Description:** Top of the 1st. You're facing the leadoff hitter — a contact guy who gets on base. What's your approach?
- **Options:**
  1. First-pitch fastball for a strike
  2. Start with a curveball to surprise him
  3. Throw a changeup to set up your fastball
  4. Waste a pitch high and inside
- **Best Answer:** Option 1 — First-pitch fastball for a strike
- **Explanations:**
  1. Great! Getting ahead 0-1 is huge. Pitchers who throw first-pitch strikes have ERAs nearly 2 runs lower.
  2. Risky to start with a breaking ball — if it hangs, he'll jump on it.
  3. Changeups work best when he expects fastball. First pitch, he doesn't know yet.
  4. Wasting pitches against a leadoff hitter just drives up your pitch count.
- **Success Rates:** 85, 40, 50, 30

- **Options:**
  1. Fastball down the middle — challenge him
  2. Slider low and away — make him chase
  3. Changeup — keep him off balance
  4. Inside fastball — brush him back
- **Best Answer:** Option 2 — Slider low and away
- **Explanations:**
  1. Too risky! MLB hitters bat .350+ on middle-middle fastballs in high-leverage spots.
  2. Perfect! The slider low-away gets a 34% whiff rate in full counts. Make him chase out of the zone.
  3. On 3-2 with bases loaded, a changeup that misses becomes ball four — walk-off walk.
  4. Inside on 3-2 with bases loaded? If you miss, it's a walk-off walk. Too much risk.
